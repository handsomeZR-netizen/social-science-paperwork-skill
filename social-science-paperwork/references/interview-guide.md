# Interview Guide

## Protocol Structure

A semi-structured interview protocol should include:

- Study title and purpose.
- Participant inclusion criteria.
- Participant exclusion criteria.
- Consent script.
- Recording permission.
- Right to withdraw.
- Expected duration and interview mode.
- Warm-up questions.
- Core questions mapped to RQs.
- Follow-up probes.
- Closing question.
- Debrief and withdrawal/contact information.

Use `assets/interview_protocol_template.md` as a starter.

## Question Design

Prefer open, experience-based questions:

- "Can you describe a recent project where..."
- "How did you decide..."
- "What changed when..."
- "Can you give an example..."
- "How do people in your role usually understand..."

Avoid:

- Yes/no questions unless used as transitions.
- Leading questions.
- Multiple questions in one sentence.
- Abstract theory words before the participant introduces them.
- Questions that imply the desired answer.

Ask experience-first questions before introducing the researcher's analytic categories. For example, ask "Can you walk me through a project where this happened?" before asking whether the issue is environmental, cultural, educational, or market-related.

## RQ Mapping

For any interview output, include a table:

| RQ | Interview question | Probe | Evidence sought | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|

This prevents a polished interview list from drifting away from the research questions.

## Sampling And Saturation

For qualitative research, sample size depends on diversity and saturation, not a fixed universal N. Report:

- Recruitment strategy.
- Participant roles.
- Experience range.
- Interview length.
- Data collection mode.
- Why the sample is enough for the claim.
- Whether new interviews stopped producing new themes.

## Transcription And Anonymization

Before analysis:

- Remove names, locations, organizations, phone numbers, IDs, and project-sensitive details unless permission exists.
- Replace participants with stable IDs such as P01, P02.
- Keep a separate secure key if needed.
- Mark uncertain transcript segments.
- Preserve pauses or emotion only if analytically relevant.

## AI Use

AI can help polish protocols, identify leading questions, propose probes, and summarize anonymized transcripts. It must not replace researcher interpretation, consent, or raw-data protection.
