# Section Quality Gates

Use these gates before finalizing any Premium Draft Demo manuscript.

## Global Gates

- The first page contains a visible Draft Demo disclosure.
- The paper has one clear central argument.
- Every major claim has a claim-evidence status: real input, proposed design, simulated material, or placeholder.
- Simulated excerpts use IDs such as `P01-demo`.
- Citation-bearing claims use verified citations or LaTeX TODO comments.
- No section implies completed data collection unless real data was provided.

## Abstract

Pattern: context -> gap -> approach -> illustrative evidence -> contribution -> boundary.

Gate:

- Names the demo status.
- Does not claim completed empirical findings.
- States what the draft demonstrates.

## Introduction

Pattern: broad practical tension -> field state -> bounded gap -> present demo.

Gate:

- Does not overclaim a local or target-journal gap as a field-wide gap.
- Ends with the paper's central argument and contribution.
- Avoids method details that belong in Methodology.

## Literature Review and Framework

Pattern: concept cluster 1 -> concept cluster 2 -> concept cluster 3 -> synthesis.

Gate:

- Organizes by concepts, not paper-by-paper summary.
- Defines the analytic concepts before using them.
- Ends with the exact gap the demo paper addresses.

## Research Questions

Gate:

- RQs match the method.
- Qualitative RQs ask about meaning, process, relation, or boundary.
- No causal hypothesis appears without a causal design.

## Methodology

Gate:

- Labels the section as proposed design if the study has not been conducted.
- Includes sampling, recruitment, data sources, ethics, consent, anonymization, and analysis route.
- Explains why the method fits the claim.

## Illustrative Findings

Gate:

- Starts with a warning that findings are simulated.
- Each theme has: claim-first opening, simulated excerpt, code/theme link, interpretation, boundary.
- Uses "illustrative" language, not completed-research language.
- Includes at least one tension, contradiction, or negative case.

## Discussion

Gate:

- Interprets what the demo would mean if supported by real data.
- Separates theoretical, practical, and methodological implications.
- States when the interpretation may fail.

## Conclusion

Gate:

- Restates the central contribution.
- Mentions the evidence boundary.
- Gives next real-data steps.
- Introduces no new data.

## Quality Audit

Every Premium Draft Demo package must include `quality_audit.md` with:

- Draft disclosure check.
- Claim-evidence map.
- Simulated material inventory.
- Overclaim check.
- Citation placeholder inventory.
- Demo-to-real replacement checklist.
