# Literature Review

## Review Type

Choose the review form before collecting papers:

- **Narrative review** - Build an argument from selected theoretical and empirical literature.
- **Scoping review** - Map concepts, methods, populations, and gaps across a broad field.
- **Systematic review** - Answer a focused question with reproducible search, screening, and extraction.
- **Theoretical review** - Compare concepts, frameworks, and explanatory models.
- **Bibliometric review** - Analyze metadata patterns such as authors, keywords, journals, and citations.

## Search Strategy

Record:

- Databases searched.
- Date searched.
- Search strings.
- Time span.
- Language restrictions.
- Inclusion and exclusion criteria.
- Screening counts.

For design/craft/culture topics, combine four groups:

1. Domain terms: craft, ceramics, pottery, heritage, traditional technique.
2. Context terms: contemporary design, design education, design practice.
3. Value terms: sustainability, cultural sustainability, circular design, heritage preservation.
4. Method terms: qualitative, interview, case study, ethnography, thematic analysis.

## Evidence Matrix

For every included paper, extract:

- Citation key and DOI/URL.
- Research field and journal.
- Research question or objective.
- Theory or framework.
- Method and data source.
- Sample or corpus.
- Analysis method.
- Main findings.
- Limitations.
- Relevance to current paper.
- Quote or page anchor for important claims.

Use `assets/literature_matrix_template.csv` as a starter.

## Synthesis Moves

Avoid paragraph-by-paragraph summaries. Organize by:

- Competing theoretical explanations.
- Methods and evidence types.
- Contexts and populations.
- Convergent findings.
- Contradictions.
- Missing cases or underdeveloped concepts.

Strong gap statements specify what is missing, why it matters, and how the current study can actually address it.

## AI Use

AI may summarize real abstracts or full texts, classify papers, and suggest synthesis structures. Human review must verify paper existence, citation metadata, and whether each paper supports the claim being made.
