# Draft Demo Presentation Notes

## Purpose

This presentation explains how the social-science-paperwork workflow turns a broad topic into a structured social science or humanities manuscript draft.

## What Is Real

- Topic: [user-provided topic]
- Existing materials: [literature notes, corpus results, cases, or class requirements]
- Methodological constraints: [target discipline, audience, available data]

## What Is Simulated

- Participant IDs and excerpts.
- Demonstration findings.
- Example coding decisions.
- Optional survey patterns.

## Workflow To Explain

1. Start from the research problem.
2. Choose a method route.
3. Build the conceptual framework.
4. Define RQs and evidence boundaries.
5. Draft interview, questionnaire, or case-study tools.
6. Demonstrate analysis with clearly labeled simulated material.
7. Show what must be replaced before real submission.

## Talking Points

- The demo is designed to show structure and reasoning, not completed evidence.
- A real study would need verified literature, ethical review or exemption, actual participants or cases, anonymized data, and documented analysis.
- The value of the workflow is that every claim is linked to the kind of evidence it would need.

## Risks Before Submission

- Simulated material must be removed or replaced.
- Citations must be verified against real sources.
- Methods must match actual data collection.
- Claims must stay within the final evidence boundary.
