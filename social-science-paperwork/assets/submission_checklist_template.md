# Social Science Submission Checklist

## Argument

- [ ] The title, abstract, keywords, research questions, and conclusion align.
- [ ] The introduction states a real gap, not just a missing topic.
- [ ] The contribution is theoretical, empirical, methodological, practical, or a clear combination.

## Method

- [ ] Research design matches the claim type.
- [ ] Sample or corpus is described with inclusion criteria.
- [ ] Recruitment and data collection dates are stated where relevant.
- [ ] Ethics approval, exemption, or institutional route is stated.
- [ ] Consent and anonymization are described for human-subject data.
- [ ] AI-assisted processing of data is disclosed if required.

## Analysis

- [ ] Qualitative coding has a traceable evidence chain.
- [ ] Quantitative analysis includes cleaning, reliability/validity checks, and model rationale.
- [ ] Claims do not exceed sample and design limits.
- [ ] Negative or deviant cases are handled where relevant.

## Reporting

- [ ] Tables and figures are numbered, cited, and interpretable.
- [ ] Instruments, codebook, or search strategy are included as appendix/supplement where appropriate.
- [ ] References are cited in text and present in the bibliography.
- [ ] Citation style follows APA 7, GB/T 7714, or target journal style consistently.
